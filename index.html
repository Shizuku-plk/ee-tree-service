Topping trees is not a good idea as it provides no benefit to the tree. In fact, topping trees causes:
  1. Starvation: Topping removes large portions of the crown, reducing the tree's ability to manufacture energy resources to recover from the pruning cuts.
  2. Insects, diseases, and decay: Large pruning woundes are vulnerable to insect invasion and the spores of wood-decaying organisms.
  3. Weak branch attachments: A new branch that sprouts after a large one is shortened is more weakly attached than a branch that develops normally.
  4. Tree death: Many trees do not have the ability or resources to recover from this extreme pruning, leading to decline and eventual death.

The best time to trim trees is during the dormant season which is typically between late fall and early spring.
